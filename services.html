Services

Different jobs need different kinds of writing.

I write across a range of formats, from websites and articles to white papers, press releases and longer pieces of content.

Some briefs need a clear commercial tone. Some need structure, research and a steadier hand. Some just need the writing stripped back so it says what it ought to say.

The format changes. The job stays much the same: write clearly, write properly, and make it work for the audience it is meant for.

01

Website copy

Copy for homepages, about pages, service pages and full websites. Clear, readable writing that helps people understand what you do and why they should care.

  • Homepages and landing pages
  • Service and product pages
  • About pages and brand messaging
  • Rewriting or tightening existing copy

02

Articles and blog writing

Blog posts, features and articles written to inform, explain or persuade, depending on what the brief calls for.

  • Business blogs and thought pieces
  • Editorial articles and features
  • SEO-aware content that still reads naturally
  • Ghostwritten content for brands and individuals

03

White papers and long-form content

Longer pieces that need structure, clarity and a steady tone throughout. Useful when a subject needs proper explanation rather than a quick skim over the surface.

  • White papers
  • Reports and research-led pieces
  • Guides and longer downloadable content
  • Content that needs shaping into a readable format

04

Press releases and promotional copy

Writing that needs to make its point quickly, whether that is for media, marketing or something in between.

  • Press releases
  • Leaflets and brochures
  • Promotional copy
  • Campaign support materials

05

Editorial and journalism-led work

Work that benefits from a journalistic background, whether that means finding the angle, making sense of information, or keeping the writing sharp and readable.

  • Interview-based writing
  • Features and comment pieces
  • Research-led editorial work
  • Content that needs a clear narrative thread

06

Scriptwriting

Writing for spoken delivery needs a different rhythm. I’ve worked on scripts as well as page-based copy, which helps when something needs to sound right as well as read well.

  • Broadcast-related writing
  • Script development
  • Writing for spoken word formats
  • Copy that needs a natural, conversational tone

How I work

Usually, the job starts with working out what the writing actually needs to do.

Sometimes that means writing from scratch. Sometimes it means improving what is already there. Either way, the first step is getting clear on the audience, the tone and the point of the piece.

I can work from a rough brief, a set of notes, a conversation, or a half-finished draft that needs sorting out. The aim is the same: end up with something that reads naturally and does the job properly.

Rates and scope depend on the brief, the length, and the amount of research or development involved.

In short
